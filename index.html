About

Boxiang Zhang is a Ph.D. candidate in Machine Learning and Data Science at Purdue University. His research focuses on efficient and deployable AI systems, especially transformer compression and post-training optimization .

He develops structured pruning methods for Vision Transformers, including one-shot approaches that preserve representations without retraining. His work shows strong efficiency gains with minimal accuracy loss, enabling practical deployment of large models .

His experience spans computer vision, anomaly detection, and multi-modal learning, with real-world systems such as industrial defect detection achieving over 95% AUROC. He aims to bridge advanced AI research with real-world impact through efficient model design
